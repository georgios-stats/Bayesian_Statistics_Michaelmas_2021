A About Nuisance parameters

Assume observable quantities y=( y 1 ,..., y n ) . Assume that the sampling distribution is d F( y| θ ) labeled by an unknown parameter θ ∈ Θ . Let θ =( φ , λ ) ⊤ with φ ∈ Φ and λ ∈ Λ . Assume You are interested in learning parameter φ ∈ Φ , and You are not interested in learning the unknown parameter λ ∈ Λ ; but both φ , λ are parts of the statistical model parameterisation. The unknown quantity λ ∈ Λ is called nuisance parameter. We an call φ ∈ Φ parameter of interest.
Note 7. In Bayesian Stats, learning (or quantifying uncertainty about) parameter of interest φ under the presence of a nuisance parameter λ ∈ Λ is performed according to the Bayesian paradigm as usual: You specify a prior d Π ( φ , λ ) with PDF/PMF π ( φ , λ ) = π ( φ | λ ) π ( λ ) on the joint space of ALL Your unknown parameters θ =( φ , λ ) ⊤ ; you compute the joint posterior distribution d Π ( θ |y ) of θ =( φ , λ ) ⊤ via the Bayesian theorem. Reasonably, Your posterior degree of believe about the parameter of interest φ given the data y=( 1 1 ,..., y n ) is given through the marginal posterior distribution d Π ( φ |y ) .

General:
